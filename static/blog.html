Pancakes

Vegan pancakes, made easy! These 3-ingredient vegan pancakes require just a blender and a pan, and the batter is ready in just a few minutes! Plus, they are gluten-free, oil-free, and perfect for meal prep. These and Breakfast at Tiffany's are the perfect warm bfast for xmas holidays. ... All you need for these pancakes are 3 simple ingredients:

  • 3/4 cups (54 grams) Rolled Oats: If you are gluten-free, use gluten-free rolled oats.
  • 1 big Ripe Banana: The riper the bananas are, the sweeter they get!
  • 1/2 cup (125 mL) Plant-Based Milk: I use unsweetened soya just because itâ€™s usually what I have, but feel free to use any plant-based milk of your choice! You can even use a sweetened and/or vanilla variety to give the pancakes that extra oomph.
To take these pancakes to the next level, consider adding things like:
  • Cinnamon or Pumpkin Pie Spice
  • Vanilla Extract
  • Chocolate Chips
  • Nuts and seeds
  • Blueberries
Blend and pour the batter into a greased pan! (I reccomend using coconut oil). Allow the pancake to cook until bubbles appear, then flip and cook until golden!

Tropical Smoothie Bowl

Perfect summer breakfast to refresh your body. Smoothie bowls can be a good source of fiber, protein, healthy fats and carbohydrates but can quickly tuen into a high-calorie sugar bomb if ingredients are not chosen wisely. Here's some inspo of a well-balanced SB! ...

  1. Choose water,coconut water or 100% fresh fruit juice as your liquid base (23 tbsp). I chose freshly squeezed orange juice for this one.
  2. Select a variety of frozen fruit + veg: I chose pink dragon fruit, but you can add more than one fruit. 1 cup or 1/2 and 1/2
  3. Add in a good source of protein: chia seeds, flax seeds, hemp seeds, protein powders (1 scoop), spirolina, chlorella, collagen, sunflowe seeds or pumpkin seeds. I chose to go with chia seeds beacuse it was what I had (1tbsp).
  4. Use some herbs and spices: cacao powder, maca, turmeric, ginger...
  5. Choose delicious toppings: nut butters, diced fruit, seeds, shredded coconut, popped quinoa, prebiotic cereal (but don't go crazy!)
Blend all the ingredients and make it pretty!!

Very Berry Oats

Ah berries!.. so rich in fiber, vitamins C and A and powerful antioxidants. They combine perfectly with your morning oats and here's how to make them: ...

  • 1/2 cup of old fashioned oats
  • 1/2 cup water or plant-based milk
  • 1/2 cup of frozen berries
I also like to add in 1 tbsp of chia seeds for added healthy omega-3 fats, protein, fiber and calcium
Instructions:
  1. In a small pot, stir oats, chosen liquid, berries and seeds over medium heat
  2. When the muxture starts to bubble, crush the berries with the back of the spoon and turn the heat down to low and cook until oats are tender to your preference, stirring occasionally.
  3. Pour the oatmeal into a bowl and top with more berries, seeds, nut butters, shredded coconut... I added sunflower seeds for added vitamin E, B6, iron, zinc and selenium and more berries beacuse you can never get too much :)

Glass Noodle stir fry

Glass noodles are easy to make and ook quickly- they can be soaked in warm water for 10 minutes or boiled for 5 minutes.... These noodles are a lighter alternative to regular pasta as they are typicalluy made from sweet potato or mung bean starch. They don't have a strong flavor and are a pretty neutral tasting noodle making them great for a lot of dishes. They soak up liquids very well so don't be shy when adding sauces to them!

  1. In a pot, boil the noodles and once ready, drain and set aside
  2. In a large nonstick skillet, heat the vegetable oil with the sesame oil. Add the snap peas, carrots, red pepper, red onion, yellow squash, zucchini and scallions and season with salt and pepper. Stir-fry the vegetables over high heat until lightly browned in spots but still crisp-tender, about 3 minutes
  3. Add the soaked glass noodles to the skillet and stir-fry over high heat until softened, about 2 minutes. Add the rice vinegar and orange juice and continue stir-frying until the glass noodles are translucent, about 2 minutes. Transfer the noodle stir-fry to plates and serve hot.
Add ginger and chilli flakes for extra flavour!!

Bliss balls

Three reasons why bliss balls should be your go-to snack: ...

  • They are full of nutritional benefits- created with a base of nuts and seeds they contain a lot of healthy fats and are rich in fibre and protein (good source of vitamin E and B2)
  • They are the perfect sweet treat after a meal, they serve as a pre-workout snack and can serve as a quick bfast on the go
  • They can be prepared in advance - you only need 10 minute sand they can last in the fridge for up to two weeks!
Made these bliss balls the other day and they were sooo good!! They're refined sugar-free and naturally sweetened with dates :
  • 1/2 cup chia seeds
  • 1 cup gluten free oats
  • 2 tbsp raw cacao powder
  • 1/4 to 1/2 tsp salt
  • 3/4 cup dates previously soaked
  • 1 to 2 tbsp water
You can customize them however you like: add protein powder, carrots, nut butter... (makes 8-9 balls) Pulse the ingredients in a food processor until a sticky mixture forms. Add a splash of water if necessary. Form balls and roll in unsweetened shredded coconut. Place in the freezer for at least 30 minutes and enjoy !!
